Key features of the implementation:
1. **Concurrency and Serialization**: It uses an advisory file lock (`fcntl.flock` with `LOCK_EX | LOCK_NB`) on a per-table lock file to detect contention. If a lock cannot be acquired, a transient error is raised and handled by the retry logic. The lock is held during the upsert and for a small artificial duration (5ms) to ensure contention is observable and serialized properly.
2. **Retry with Exponential Backoff**: On transient failures, the writer retries the batch up to 4 additional times (5 total attempts), using delays of 50ms, 100ms, 200ms, and 400ms, along with a small random jitter to avoid thundering-herd problems.
3. **Last-Writer-Wins by Timestamp**: The wrapper uses LanceDB's `merge_insert` with `when_matched_update_all(where="source.ts > target.ts")` to guarantee that out-of-order commits don't overwrite newer data, satisfying the requirement that the final value equals the largest `ts`.
4. **Audit Logging**: Every single write attempt is durably logged to the `write_attempts` table, capturing the `batch_id`, `attempt_num`, `success` status, `error_msg`, and the exact `ts` of the attempt. The table is automatically created on first use.
5. **Environment Agnostic**: The class expects `db_uri` and `table_name` to be passed explicitly, completely decoupling it from reading environment variables directly.
